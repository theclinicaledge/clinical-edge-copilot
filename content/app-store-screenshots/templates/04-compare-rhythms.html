Feature Comparison
Rate
150–250 bpm — very fast, often sudden
101–180 bpm — rate reflects physiologic drive
Onset
Abrupt — "flips on" suddenly, often felt as palpitations
Gradual — rises with activity, pain, or fever
P Waves
Hidden or retrograde — buried in T wave
Visible, upright, one before each QRS
QRS
Narrow < 0.12 s (unless aberrant)
Narrow < 0.12 s, normal morphology
Cause
Re-entry circuit — not a physiologic response
Hypoxia, pain, hypovolemia, fever — always has a cause
The key distinction
If the rate is very fast and the patient says it came on suddenly — think SVT. If there's a reason for the heart to be fast (pain, fever, bleeding) — think sinus tach. Treat the cause.
