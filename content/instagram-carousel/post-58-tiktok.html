Know what

Possible concerns and targeted assessments — structured for the bedside.

Clinical Edge
COPILOT
Structured clinical reasoning support — confirm with your assessment
URGENCY: HIGH
What this could be
A sudden BP drop with compensatory tachycardia after a stable period points toward an acute hemodynamic shift worth investigating at the bedside.
What to assess next
›Mental status — confusion, agitation, or unusual quietness changes the picture significantly
›Skin perfusion — mottling, pallor, extremity temperature, and capillary refill
›Work of breathing — new tachypnea or accessory muscle use alongside this picture
›What changed in the last hour — procedure, new medication, fluid shift, or new complaint
›Volume status clues — dry mucous membranes, flat neck veins, recent output vs. intake

What to assess next
›Mental status — orientation and responsiveness
›Skin perfusion — temperature, mottling, capillary refill
›Respiratory rate and work of breathing
Where this may be heading
›If this reflects early shock, the tachycardia compensating it may not hold indefinitely
›Mentation changes are often the next signal in progressive hypoperfusion
›A patient who dropped quickly after stability may be on a trajectory the team wants to get ahead of
›Local protocol and provider guidance come first
