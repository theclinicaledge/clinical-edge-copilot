What this could be

The cause matters. Don’t skip past it.

  • Fatigue after prolonged agitation
  • Change in mentation or level of consciousness
  • Evolving hypoxia or perfusion issue
  • Medication effect finally hitting

What concerns me most

The shift was abrupt. Abrupt means something.

  • Behavioral change with no clear cause
  • “Calm” that isn’t oriented or engaged
  • Loss of interaction or response to voice
  • Looks better on the surface, harder to reach underneath

What I’d assess next

Compare to where they were an hour ago. Not to a reference range.

  • Alertness vs earlier baseline, not just “awake”
  • Response to voice and purposeful stimulation
  • Breathing pattern, rate, and work of breathing
  • Overall appearance — color, skin, posture

Slide 06 — What I'd Do Right Now
Copilot

What I’d do right now

01

Reassess fully, not just behavior

The behavior changed. That’s the flag — not the finding.

02

Compare to earlier mental status

Were they agitated and alert? Or agitated and confused? Different picture.

03

Stay present and observe closely

Don’t step out. Watch breathing, response, position.

04

Escalate if the change doesn’t make sense

“I’m not sure why they stopped” is a valid reason to notify.

What matters here

Not all calm is improvement

Quiet and better are two different things. Check which one this is.

Sudden quiet can be a warning sign

Prolonged agitation that stops abruptly often has a physiologic reason.

The why matters more than the behavior

Relief that the behavior stopped is understandable. Acting on it isn’t optional.
