Clinical Reasoning

The trend tells
the real story.

A “normal” vital sign can still be concerning when it is moving in the wrong direction.
01Respiratory rate
18→22
→26
Often changes before the monitor looks dramatic.
02Oxygen need
Room air→2 L→4 L
→6 L
The SpO₂ may look acceptable because support keeps increasing.
03Blood pressure
128/72→106/64
→92/58
The direction matters, especially with symptoms or poor perfusion.
04Mental status
Alert→restless
→confused
A new change in mental status should never be dismissed as “just tired.”
Don’t only ask:
“Is this value abnormal?”
Ask:
“What has changed, and where is this heading?”
