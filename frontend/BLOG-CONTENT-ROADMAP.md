# Clinical Edge Blog — Content Roadmap

A first-12-article plan. Each article should hit the same bar as the ABG
guide: a repeatable framework, realistic worked examples, nursing-scope
language (assessment and escalation, not independent treatment), and a
genuine tie-in to an existing Clinical Edge tool. Publish when an article is
actually ready — this list is a backlog, not a deadline.

**Suggested cadence:** one article every 2–3 weeks. Quality and clinical
accuracy compound; a thin, rushed article does more harm than good to both
trust and search performance. Twelve well-made articles over 6–9 months beats
twelve mediocre ones in six weeks.

## Notes on prioritization

- Articles 1 and 12 are natural companions to the existing ABG guide and
  reuse its internal-linking network immediately — good candidates for
  early publication while that traffic is fresh.
- Articles 2–4 (rhythm-focused) all tie to Rhythm Lab and can share a
  consistent "strip-first" visual format.
- Articles 6, 7, 9 (drip-focused) all tie to ICU Drips and can share a
  consistent nursing-considerations template.
- No search-volume numbers are claimed here — none were available to verify,
  and inventing them would be misleading. Prioritization above is based on
  topic clarity, existing product tie-in, and realistic nursing search intent,
  not projected traffic.
