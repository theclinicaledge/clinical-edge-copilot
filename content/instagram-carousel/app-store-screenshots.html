Possible concerns
› Estimated MAP ≈ 54 mmHg — well below the 65 mmHg perfusion threshold; end-organ risk is already present
› Compensatory tachycardia at 122 may not sustain perfusion — if HR drops or rises further, the picture changes fast
› Acute onset (stable 1 hour ago) suggests a sudden precipitating event — not chronic hypotension
› Potential for rapid deterioration in minutes without targeted intervention
What to assess next
› Skin perfusion — cap refill, temperature, mottling; cool/clammy = low output state
› Mental status — new confusion or agitation is an early perfusion sign
› Urine output trend — <0.5 mL/kg/hr signals hypoperfusion
› IV access — confirm large-bore patency; consider second access if single 22g
› Recent medications — anything given in the past hour that could explain the drop?
› 12-lead ECG — rule out cardiac etiology; look for ST changes, new arrhythmia
