Slide 04 — The Problem
The problem
04 / 08

You’re waiting for the lab to catch up.

Physiology changes first

The patient starts shifting before any number moves. That’s what you’re reading at the bedside.

Labs reflect it later

A lactate is a snapshot. By the time it rises, the window has already been narrowing.

Sometimes much later

Compensated shock can look unremarkable on paper for longer than most people expect.

Slide 06 — Clinical Thinking
Clinical Thinking
06 / 08

When something doesn’t match.

01

Trust the mismatch

If the patient looks worse than the chart suggests, that gap is information.

02

Reassess the patient directly

Mental status, work of breathing, skin, cap refill. What has changed since your last look?

03

Look for what changed, not what’s documented

Trend the vitals yourself. Compare to two hours ago, not the admission value.

04

Notify provider with what you’re seeing

Lead with the clinical picture, not just the number that’s normal.
